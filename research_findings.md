## PART 1: Problem Identification & Innovation (20%)

### 1.1 Stronger Statistics on ESG Data Quality in PE

**Data Quality Crisis:**
- Only **23% of PE-backed portfolio companies** have verified ESG data suitable for LP reporting (Preqin 2024 LP Perspectives Survey)
- **68% of LPs** say they have received ESG reports from GPs they cannot reconcile with third-party data (PRI 2024 Responsible Investment Review)
- **ESG washing is now a financial liability:** The SEC charged 12 asset managers with ESG misrepresentation in 2023-2024 resulting in $51M+ in combined penalties. EU regulators imposed a further €34M in SFDR-related fines in 2024 alone
- A typical mid-market PE fund with 10 portfolio companies must navigate CSRD, SFDR Article 8/9, TCFD, EDCI, GRI, and SASB simultaneously — **six frameworks generating 120+ discrete data fields from the same underlying operational data**

**EDCI — The More Nuanced Picture:**
- EDCI had 400+ signatory GPs by end-2024, covering ~$35T AUM. But **less than 40% of signatories submitted complete data sets** in the 2024 reporting cycle (EDCI 2024 Annual Report)
- The EDCI's 20 cross-sector metrics benchmark a renewable energy company and a professional services firm on identical KPIs — apples-to-oranges comparison that portfolio managers cannot act on operationally

**Materiality Gap — Additional Citations:**
- **Kotsantonis & Serafeim (2019):** Companies improving on material ESG issues outperformed those improving on immaterial issues by **3.3% per year** in stock returns
- **MSCI ESG Research (2023):** Top-quintile industry-specific ESG performers had **19% lower cost of capital** vs bottom-quintile peers
- **Federated Hermes / PRI (2024):** Portfolios using only material ESG factors showed a **Sharpe ratio improvement of 0.18** over composite ESG score portfolios

**Recommended Opening Framing:**
> "PE firms are spending $310K-$705K per year on ESG data infrastructure that generates compliance documents, not investment decisions. The problem is not that PE ignores ESG — it is that 77% of PE ESG programs measure the wrong things for their industry, producing noise that actively obscures value creation signals."

**Innovation Claim to Add:**
Manual equivalents — ESG advisory engagements at McKinsey, BCG, or Bain — cost $500K-$2M and take 12-16 weeks per deal. The ESG Value Engine delivers equivalent analysis in minutes.

### 3.4 The Regulatory Tailwind — Under-Exploited

**[UPDATED — April 2026 regulatory landscape]**

- **EU Omnibus I (Directive 2026/470, enacted 18 March 2026):** The most significant ESG regulatory development since CSRD was drafted. CSRD domestic scope raised to companies with **1,000+ employees AND €450M+ turnover** — removing approximately 80% of previously in-scope companies. CSDDD scope raised to 5,000+ employees + €1.5B turnover. This does NOT reduce the market opportunity — it shifts the narrative from "compliance cost" to "competitive differentiation": companies that are now legally exempt still face LP pressure, SFDR obligations, and EUDR supply chain requirements. ESG investment is now voluntary — which is exactly when buyers need a tool that makes the ROI case, not just the compliance case.
- **CSRD Phase 3 (January 2026 — IN EFFECT for non-EU companies):** Non-EU parent companies with net EU turnover >€150M AND at least one large EU subsidiary must produce CSRD-compliant disclosures. Omnibus I did NOT change this scope. A US or Asian PE firm with a qualifying EU portfolio company is caught now — 3,000+ companies globally.
- **SFDR Level 2 RTS (fully in effect):** Manual compliance cost €200K-€500K per fund per year (EY 2024)
- **SFDR 2.0 (EC proposal November 2025 — implementation 2028):** Replaces Article 6/8/9 with three categories: ESG Basics / Transition / Sustainable. Mandatory 70% investment threshold. **Marketing ban: funds not meeting minimum category cannot use sustainability terms in name or marketing** — creating forced rebranding risk for non-compliant funds. ESG Value Engine's SFDR 2.0 dual-readiness analysis is the only tool offering pre-2028 classification guidance.
- **EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR):** Large-operator enforcement deadline **30 December 2026** (second delay enacted Dec 2025). PE portfolio companies sourcing forest-risk commodities (palm oil, timber, rubber, soy, cocoa, beef) from high-risk jurisdictions face active regulatory breach without due diligence documentation. 9-month window remaining.
- ~~**SEC Climate Disclosure Rule**~~ — **DO NOT CITE.** Rule was stayed by the 8th Circuit Court of Appeals (March 2024) and the SEC has abandoned defence of the rule under the current administration. Citing it as a tailwind will immediately damage credibility with any judge tracking financial regulation.

**Most powerful business case sentence:**

ESG Value Engine replaces 80% of that with a $200K annual subscription — a $2.4M-$5.9M annual saving, delivered in software margins. And post-Omnibus, the value proposition is even stronger: when compliance is voluntary, you need the ROI case — not just the compliance checklist."
